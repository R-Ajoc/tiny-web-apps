Nothanger Abbey

Northanger Abbey is a satirical novel by Jane Austen, published posthumously in 1817. It follows the story of Catherine Morland as she navigates her first visit to Bath, where she meets new friends and becomes infatuated with the mysterious Henry Tilney. The novel humorously critiques the Gothic novel genre and explores themes of imagination, social class, and the nature of love.
